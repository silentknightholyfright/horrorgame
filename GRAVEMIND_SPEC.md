## Core Concept

You are not the victim — you are the nightmare.

Captured by a secret human organization, you awaken inside a shifting underground facility designed to suppress your abilities. But something went wrong. The systems are failing… and you are evolving.

You are a mysterious, non-human entity capable of manipulating antigravity — lifting, crushing, and distorting the environment itself. As containment weakens, you must escape. But the deeper you go, the more you uncover about why you were captured… and what you truly are.

The central question running through every ending:

> *"If something powerful enough has no choice in what it becomes — is it a monster, or a victim?"*

---

## Tone & Atmosphere

- Dark, oppressive sci-fi facility
- Body horror and psychological horror elements
- Sound design driven by distant screams, alarms, and distorted gravity effects
- You are powerful — but never fully in control
- Lights pulse in rhythm with your heartbeat; the building *knows* you are there
- Silence as horror — the scariest moments are when everything goes quiet

## Core Mechanics

### Antigravity Manipulation

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Lift** | Raise enemies into the air, rendering them helpless |
| **Slam** | Hurl suspended objects or enemies into walls |
| **Gravity Well** | Create a localized zone that pulls everything inward; useful for trapping groups or collapsing structures |
| **Inversion Corridor** | Flip gravity in an entire hallway temporarily — guards fall to the ceiling, loose objects become projectiles |
| **Suspension Interrogation** | Hold a guard in mid-air; they will reveal patrol routes, trap locations, or lore |
| **Gravity Echoes** | Leave permanent reality-warped scars in areas where power has been used heavily |

### Predator Vision

| Sense | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Fear Mapping** | Heatmap of guard terror levels; high fear zones signal traps or incoming reinforcements |
| **Heartbeat Detection** | Sense movement and heartbeat through walls |
| **Memory Echoes** | Touch a dead guard to briefly see their last moments — useful for intel but humanising |
| **Structural Sight** | See facility weak points — pipes, load-bearing walls, power conduits |
| **Psychic Noise** | As facility-wide fear increases, your own vision distorts — power comes at a cost |

### Environmental Horror

- Lights flicker and gravity becomes unstable as you move through zones
- Corridors physically shift in response to your presence
- The facility reacts to you emotionally, not just mechanically

## Terror Strike System

Terror Strikes are deliberate, skill-based actions that weaponize fear. They are not just kills — they are psychological tools that feed your evolution and reshape the facility.

### Types of Terror Strikes

| Strike | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **The Ambush** | Phase through a wall or ceiling, appear directly in a guard's face. Distorted sound blast; guard reacts based on sanity level |
| **The Reveal** | Stand perfectly still in the dark. Move as the guard turns to leave — triggers squad-wide panic |
| **The Drop** | Suspend on the ceiling using antigravity, then drop silently behind a guard passing beneath |
| **The Emergence** | Crawl slowly out of a flooded floor, vent, or through a wall. The slower the emergence, the higher the terror payoff |
| **The Mirror** | *(Psychological path only)* Briefly take on the silhouette of someone the guard knows. Reveal your true form as they approach. Maximum psychological damage |

### Fear Threshold Stages

| Stage | Guard Behaviour |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Shaken** | Misses shots, breathes heavily, slows down |
| **Panicked** | Flees, screams, alerts nearby guards |
| **Broken** | Catatonic, drops weapons, useless to WARD |
| **Cardiac Failure** | Instant incapacitation — no physical contact required |
| **Witness Contamination** | Guards who *hear* another's terror reaction receive a partial fear bump; fear spreads like a virus |

### Player Skill Elements

- **Timing Window** — a brief prompt appears at perfect positioning; hit it for a full Terror Strike
- **Stillness Mechanic** — the longer you hold completely still before striking, the more powerful the scare
- **Sound Baiting** — knock objects or distort gravity nearby to lure guards into your ideal position
- **Audience Multiplier** — performing a Terror Strike while multiple guards can see it multiplies fear across all of them

### Sensory Design

- Brief slow-motion shift from your perspective — almost like savouring it
- Audio inverts: the guard's scream sounds muffled and distant while your ambient hum grows louder
- Screen edges darken — predator tunnel vision locking in
- You see the guard's face up close. This is intentional. It should feel uncomfortable.

## Adaptive Evolution System

Evolution is not just a power upgrade — it is an **identity system**. Each playstyle shapes what you are *becoming*, and the ending you receive reflects those choices.

### Evolution Paths

| Path | Unlocked By | Physical Change | Signature Power |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Brutal** | Performing Slam and Ambush strikes | Increasingly monstrous form; guards react with pure terror | Raw power upgrades; environmental destruction |
| **Stealth** | Performing Reveal and Drop strikes | Remains closer to humanoid | Briefly mimic human voices or appearance to manipulate guards |
| **Psychological** | Performing Mirror strikes | Fluid, shifting silhouette | Enter guard hallucinations, make guards turn on each other, weaponise guilt |

### Evolution Unlocks via Terror Strikes

| Enough of This Strike | Unlocks |
|-----------------------|---------|
| Ambush | **Phasing** — move through walls faster |
| Reveal | **Omnipresence** — project shadow duplicates to appear in multiple places |
| Mirror | **Mimicry** — hold human form for short periods |

### The Cost

- Brutal path: lose fragments of memory and humanity as power grows
- Each evolution is permanent and visible — the guards, the facility, and you all change

## Human AI & Faction Design

### WARD (Working name — alternatives: SABLE, Project LIMINAL)

WARD is the organisation that captured and studied you. It is not a monolith — internal divisions create unpredictable dynamics.

| Faction | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Containment Units** | Disciplined soldiers; follow protocol strictly |
| **Research Staff** | Non-combat, terrified, carry the most lore; some may attempt to communicate with you |
| **The Architect** | The brilliant, calm antagonist who designed the facility; always one step ahead; the final cat-and-mouse |
| **Defectors** | A small group who believe you are sentient and should not be caged; leave supplies but cannot be fully trusted |

### Guard Behaviour Evolution

| Game Phase | Guard Behaviour |
|------------|-----------------|
| **Early** | Confident, by-the-book patrols |
| **Mid** | Deploy anti-gravity dampeners, form tighter squads, eliminate lone targets |
| **Late** | Full psychological breakdown — some desert, some become fanatical, some attempt to reason with you |

### The Breaking Point Mechanic

Each guard has a sanity threshold. Push them past it and they become unpredictable — either more dangerous to WARD, or completely non-functional.

## World & Level Design

### The Facility as a Character

Each zone reflects a phase of your captivity. The further you descend, the more reality deteriorates.

| Zone | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Intake** | Clean, clinical, hospital-like. False sense of normalcy |
| **Suppression Wing** | Heavy machinery designed specifically to dampen your powers. Most oppressive section |
| **The Archive** | Records of your captivity. Most lore-dense area |
| **The Anomaly Floor** | Your power has been leaking here for weeks. Gravity is completely broken. Most surreal section |
| **The Surface Breach** | The exit — but what is waiting outside? |

## Narrative & Lore

### The Origin Mystery

- You were not created — you were *extracted*. Something pulled from a dimension where physics operates differently, then reverse-engineered into a body.
- WARD has done this before. You are not Subject 1. The others did not wake up… or did they?
- The containment breach was not an accident. Someone inside the facility released you — a whistleblower, a splinter faction, or another entity communicating through the failing systems.

### The Identity Horror

- Scattered logs reveal you had a *name* before. A human name. Photographs. A life.
- Were you a person who was *transformed* into this? Or is that a false memory implanted to make you easier to control?
- Late-game: you may find the original "you" — still alive, still human, locked in a cell. What you are is a copy. What do you do?

### Story Hook

Logs and fragmented memories reveal that you may not be an alien — but something humans created. And you were never meant to wake up.

## Endings

### Ending Overview

| Ending | Trigger | Tone |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Escape & Fade** | Escape without fully evolving | Bittersweet, uncertain |
| **Ascension** | Absorb the facility at full power | Terrifying, transcendent |
| **The Truth Ending** | Discover original human self | Tragic, identity-breaking |
| **Complicity Ending** | Realise you were always a weapon aimed outward | Morally devastating |
| **Containment Restored** ☠️ | Reckless play; blindly trust Defectors | Bleak, hollow, haunting |

---

### Ending Details

#### Escape & Fade
You reach the surface. The world outside is vast and unprepared. You disappear into it — not triumphant, not free, just *loose*. The final shot lingers on the horizon. No resolution. Only consequence.

#### Ascension
You do not escape the facility. You *absorb* it. The walls, the machines, the gravity arrays — all of it collapses inward and becomes part of you. You are no longer inside the building. You *are* the building. And you are expanding.

#### The Truth Ending
You find a locked cell deep in the Archive. Inside is a person — your face, your name from the logs. Human. Fragile. Awake. You are a copy. An extraction. The original was always here. The game does not tell you what to do next.

#### Complicity Ending
In the final moments, you understand: the Defectors are real, but their goal was never your freedom. You were always a weapon — aimed at a target beyond the facility walls. The breach was a launch. Do you stop yourself? Can you?

#### Containment Restored ☠️ — *The Bad Ending*

**How you get here:**
- Played recklessly — overused powers, triggered too many alarms
- Trusted the Defectors completely without questioning their motives
- Ignored all fragmented memory logs
- Fear Meter maxed out facility-wide too early, triggering WARD's **Extinction Protocol**

**The Sequence:**

You reach what appears to be the Surface Breach. The Defectors are waiting, looking relieved. But the gravity in the room is *too stable*. Your Predator Vision is being jammed.

The Architect's voice comes over the intercom — calm as always:

*"You were never escaping. You were being herded."*

The final corridor was a constructed kill chamber. The Defectors were WARD operatives running a controlled experiment — cataloguing how far you would evolve before termination. Every choice you made was observed.

The room floods with a gravitational inversion pulse — your own power, weaponised against you. Your antigravity begins tearing you apart from the inside.

The screen slowly inverts. Darkens. One final fragmented memory — the human name from the logs. A face. Maybe yours.

Silence.

**Final Shot:** The Architect writes a single line in a report: *"Subject terminated. Evolution data preserved. Begin extraction of next candidate."*

A door opens in a clean white lab. Identical to where you woke up at the start. Something stirs in the dark.
